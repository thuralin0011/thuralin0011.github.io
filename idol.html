Description

Early life and education

General Aung San started his early life in Natmauk, Magway Region, born on February 13, 1915, attended elementary education at National High School in Natmauk, continued his high school education at Yenangyaung High School where he was known for his intelligence and leadership qualities, then pursued higher education at Rangoon University in 1933 studying English Literature, History, and Political Science, where he became actively involved in student politics as the editor of the university newspaper, president of the Rangoon University Students' Union (RUSU) in 1938, and led many student movements against British colonial rule, though he later dropped out of university to focus on his political activities in the fight for Myanmar's independence.



Career

General Aung San served as a student leader and president of the Rangoon University Students' Union, worked as a journalist and editor for numerous publications including "Oway" newspaper, became a prominent political leader as the secretary of Dobama Asiayone (We Burmans Association), served as the Commander-in-Chief of the Burma Independence Army (BIA), held positions as Deputy Chairman of the Executive Council of British-Burma Government, Minister of Defense, and leader of the Anti-Fascist People's Freedom League (AFPFL), negotiated with British government for Burma's independence, organized the historic Panglong Conference to unite ethnic minorities, and served as the interim Prime Minister of Burma until his assassination in 1947 at the age of 32.
